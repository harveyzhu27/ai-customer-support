Question 1: Is the rate variable?
Answer 1: The Aven Card is a variable rate credit card. Cash outs may have a fixed rate option. See your offer for details. The variable rate varies based on an Index (Prime Rate published in the Wall Street Journal or the Federal Funds Target Rate Upper Limit set by the Federal Reserve). This is outside Aven's control. As the Index shifts up or down, so will the APR on the Aven Card and any other variable-rate credit cards, lines, and loans. The Index may change several times a year or go for many months without change.
Source: https://www.aven.com/support

Question 2: How does Aven determine the credit line size and interest rate?
Answer 2: Aven's bank-standard underwriting system is fully automated, and calculates offers based on an applicant's income, equity, credit, and debt obligations.

Question 3: Does Aven offer a fixed rate?

Answer 4: It is both! Aven is a home equity line of credit that customers access through a credit card. Aven cardholders can use their Aven Card wherever VISA cards are accepted.

Question 5: What is an Involuntary Lien?
Answer 5: Involuntary liens are liens that are placed on a property by an outside authority against the will of the property owner such as City, County, or Federal Government Tax Liens, Mechanic's Liens, Judgement Liens, HOA Liens, Lis Pendens, Notice of Default, Notice of Sale/Foreclosure.
Source: https://www.aven.com/support

Question 6: Does my spouse have to sign?

Question 7: What is an Interspousal Transfer Deed

Source: https://www.aven.com/support

Question 8: What is a quitclaim deed?
Answer 8: A quitclaim deed is a document that is used to transfer or release a property owner’s interest to another owner. For more information, please contact the county recorder's office.
Source: https://www.aven.com/support

Question 10: A co-owner is deceased and I am trying to open an account. What should we do?

Question 11: Is there a lien on the home?

Question 13: What is a Deed of Trust?
Answer 13: A Deed of Trust is an official agreement between a lender and a borrower, stating that the borrower will repay the line of credit that is secured by the borrower's residence. This Deed of Trust is then recorded with the borrower's county as a record of the agreement.
